## Tagline (page header)

One house. Five minutes. One last snack.

## Description

You're a toddler. You skipped dinner. You regret everything.

The parent's bedtime routine is the countdown: as it winds down, the lights go
out room by room and the house falls quiet. Darkness hides you — but silence
betrays you. Every lamp that dies makes you harder to see and every sound you
make easier to hear.

Sneak out of your crib, cross the house, raid the fridge or the pantry, and
get back under the covers before the routine ends. Get spotted and you're not
out — you're just carried back to bed while your snack lies on the floor
where you dropped it. The clock never stops.

## How it reads the theme

The countdown is the parent's evening: four phases, each turning off a light
or silencing a room. The house gets darker — safer to be seen in — and
quieter — riskier to move in — as time runs out. You are always racing the
routine, never a timer bar.

## How to play

- Sneak everywhere by default. Carpet is silent. Hardwood whispers. Creaky
  boards and squeaky toys are alarms with a floor's shape.
- The pink rings are truth: they show exactly how far your sound reaches. If
  a ring touches the parent, they heard it.
- Your own body is the light meter — glowing blue means exposed, dim means
  hidden. Standing in darkness inside their view is safe. That's the promise.
- The fridge is the light gamble; the pantry is the noise gamble. Opening
  slowly is a long quiet risk, rushing is one loud flash. Release to pause a
  door mid-creak.
- Once you grab the snack, the wrapper crinkles forever. The way home is a
  different game.

## Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| WASD / Arrows | Sneak (default) |
| Hold Shift | Run — fast, loud, and the parent has a temper |
| Hold E | Open doors — hold Shift too to rush them |
| R | Restart after the end screen |
